Bai-Perron / PELT penalty sensitivity check
============================================
Data source: GitHub repo sasikiranaaz/The-Sustainability-Governance-Paradox,
             The_Sustainability_Governance_Paradox_Dataset/network properties/{density,modularity,nodes,avg weighted degree}.csv
Observations: 216 monthly observations per series, Jan 2007 - Dec 2024
Series:       12 (3 commodities x 4 metrics)
Transforms:   logit for density and modularity, log(x+1) for avg weighted degree, identity for node count
Penalty:      beta = c * sigma_hat^2 * log(n), with sigma_hat^2 from residuals of a fitted linear trend
Algorithm:    PELT (ruptures package), L2 cost, minimum segment length = 12 months
Penalty grid: c in {0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0}


Total break counts
------------------
c = 0.5  ->  48 breaks (baseline + 14)
c = 1.0  ->  34 breaks (baseline)
c = 1.5  ->  32 breaks (baseline -  2)
c = 2.0  ->  25 breaks (baseline -  9)

Range: [25, 48].  Magnitude of change vs. baseline: from -9 to +14.

Critical observation: every break in the SDG window is dated December 2014
(coinciding with the OPEC November 2014 decision and the oil-price collapse)
or May 2015 (the c = 0.5 coal modularity break, which disappears at all higher
penalty values). No break is dated to a post-SDG month other than May 2015.
